Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 2025

At DarkVault, we prioritize your privacy and security. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information when you use our platform. By using DarkVault, you agree to the terms outlined below.

1. Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information to provide and improve our services:

  • Account Information: When you register as a user or vendor, we collect your username, hashed password, Bitcoin address (for transactions), and PGP public key (for encrypted communication).
  • Transaction Data: We store details of your orders, including product IDs, Bitcoin amounts, escrow addresses, and transaction statuses, to facilitate secure exchanges.
  • Usage Data: We log your interactions with the site, such as page views, search queries, and timestamps, to enhance functionality and detect fraud.
  • Communication Data: Messages sent via our messaging system are stored, with individual messages encrypted using your PGP key.

2. How We Use Your Information

Your information is used solely to operate and secure DarkVault:

  • Service Delivery: To process orders, manage escrow, and facilitate communication between buyers, vendors, and admins.
  • Security: To detect and prevent fraud, abuse, or illegal activities using usage and transaction data.
  • Improvement: To analyze usage patterns and improve site features.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws or respond to lawful requests (though we aim to minimize such disclosures).

4. Sharing Your Information

We limit data sharing to the following scenarios:

  • Transaction Parties: Buyers and vendors share Bitcoin addresses and encrypted messages as needed for orders.
  • Admins: Site administrators access data to resolve disputes or manage the platform.

5. Your Rights and Choices

You have control over your data:

  • Account Deletion: Contact support to delete your account and associated data.
  • PGP Encryption: Provide and update your PGP key to ensure secure communication.
  • Opt-Out: You can stop using DarkVault at any time, though past transaction data may persist on the blockchain.

6. Cookies and Tracking

DarkVault does not use cookies, web beacons, or third-party tracking technologies. Session management is handled server-side with minimal data collection.

Bitcoin transactions are recorded on a public blockchain. While we anonymize your identity on our platform, blockchain data (e.g., addresses, amounts) is permanent and publicly accessible. Use wallet best practices (e.g., new addresses per transaction) to enhance your privacy.
